# Personal Homepage Redesign Specification

## Document Purpose
- This document defines the target specification for the redesigned personal homepage.
- It combines reverse-engineered facts from the current site with the clarified strategic positioning.
- It should contain enough information to rewrite the site later without reopening basic questions about audience, goals, content priorities, structure, tone, or interaction.

## Product Goal
- The site is a personal homepage used for a job search.
- Its primary purpose is to position Enrico Huettig as a strong candidate for architect-level and technical-lead roles.
- The homepage must make the hiring value explicit, not merely implied.
- The site must work for both non-technical and technical readers:
  - fast orientation for recruiters
  - credible technical proof for hiring managers

## Primary Conversion Goal
- The ideal outcome is that a visitor contacts Enrico to discuss a software architect or technical lead role.

## Secondary Conversion Goals
- A recruiter should quickly decide that Enrico is worth interviewing.
- A technical hiring manager should explore selected proof points and conclude that Enrico can handle complex systems responsibly.
- A visitor should be able to move from summary-level information into deeper project proof without friction.

## Target Audience

### Primary Audience
- Recruiters with little or no technical background.
- Hiring managers looking for a software architect, technical lead, or senior technical contributor.

### Secondary Audience
- Team leads, heads of engineering, or department leads with moderate to strong technical knowledge.
- Technical peers who want to inspect proof of thinking, quality standards, and project depth.

## Target Positioning

### Target Roles
- Primary: `Software Architect`
- Secondary: `Team Lead`
- Tertiary: `Senior Software Developer`

### Preferred Professional Framing
- Primary label near the top of the homepage should foreground architecture.
- The preferred label is: `Software Architect and Team Lead`
- The site may also communicate senior software development experience, but it must not lead with that.

### Positioning Hierarchy
- First: architect / systems thinker
- Second: deep technical builder
- Third: bridge between business and engineering

### Leadership Positioning
- The homepage should present Enrico as someone who prefers technical leadership first.
- Formal people management should not be positioned as the current primary goal.
- Leading a small team can be presented as a natural future evolution, not the first hiring message.

## Desired First Impression

### Within 10 Seconds
- A recruiter should understand:
  - this is an experienced and capable senior person
  - he has high standards
  - he understands both software and people

### Within 30 to 60 Seconds
- A technical hiring manager should understand:
  - this is a senior engineer with architect-level thinking
  - he can bring structure to complex software
  - he improves the way a team works, not just the code
  - he is strongest where complexity, quality, stakeholder communication, and long-term maintainability all matter at the same time

## Target Work Environment

### Preferred Company and Team Characteristics
- Flat hierarchies
- Small teams, ideally 3 to 5 people
- High standards and high quality expectations
- New challenges are welcome
- Mistakes are tolerated as part of learning
- Flexible work environment
- International team context is desirable

### Preferred Work Setup
- Based in Germany
- Preferred: remote or hybrid
- Open to relocation later

### Preferred Domain Direction
- B2B products
- B2C products
- Desktop software
- Developer tools

### Domain Transition Story
- The site should state directly that Enrico has worked in industrial automation for more than 25 years.
- It should also state directly that he wants to apply this depth in new areas.
- The transition should be framed positively:
  - long experience in a demanding technical field
  - now looking for new areas to explore

### Less Preferred or Avoided Direction
- The site should avoid attracting roles centered on:
  - pure coding only
  - teaching-heavy roles with many juniors
  - travel-heavy assignments
  - frequent project switching
  - cloud-first SaaS positioning as the main identity

## Core Value Proposition
- Enrico is valuable where software is complex, quality matters, and teams need calm technical leadership.
- He combines long-term technical depth with architectural thinking, process awareness, and a strong understanding of stakeholders and colleagues.
- He is not merely a senior programmer; he is strongest in shaping systems, quality, and collaboration.

## Key Differentiators
- Can listen without getting captured by the emotions of the discussion.
- Strong observer of patterns in both software work and human interaction.
- Very strong memory for details, comments, and requirements over long periods.
- Patient teacher and explainer in one-to-one or small-group contexts.
- Able to break down complexity into smaller parts without losing structural integrity.

## Evidence Behind Differentiators

### Listening Without Ego
- In emotionally charged technical discussions with stakeholders, Enrico can separate emotion from substance.
- He works toward the root problem and helps resolve the discussion so that people feel understood.

### Observation
- He recognizes recurring patterns in how people work that may indicate deeper process or team problems.
- He notices subtle reactions during reviews or discussions that may reveal hidden concerns.

### Memory
- He remembers small stakeholder comments or requirements even when they were mentioned many months earlier.
- This helps preserve important intent that might otherwise get lost.

### Patience
- He supports colleagues over longer periods when they struggle with a topic.
- He explains difficult subjects repeatedly and in simpler terms until they become useful.

### Complexity Handling
- He breaks complex problems into smaller manageable parts.
- He works in a way that preserves requirements and architectural integrity while reducing complexity.

## Proof Points To Highlight

### Primary Proof Point: Green-Field Architecture
- A communication library for the semiconductor industry implementing a standardized protocol.
- Enrico shaped major architectural decisions:
  - programming language
  - third-party dependency strategy
  - public API design
  - threading concept
  - exception and error handling
  - technical and business logging
  - threat management
- The homepage should communicate the resulting value:
  - compatibility with many customer development environments
  - low dependency risk
  - easy and safe API usage
  - room for backward compatibility and future extension
  - predictable runtime behavior
  - consistent error behavior
  - integration into existing logging frameworks
  - reduced security risk through focused, consistent concepts

### Secondary Proof Point: Quality Practices
- Automated tests were introduced from the start of a green-field project.
- Test coverage was kept above 80% continuously.
- Testing was part of the architectural decisions, not an afterthought.
- SonarQube issues were kept at a low level over a long period.
- The site should frame this as long-term quality discipline, not checkbox compliance.

### Tertiary Proof Point: Brown-Field Modernization
- Existing equipment-control software in the semiconductor industry, approximately 25 years old.
- Major improvements included:
  - migration from a proprietary internal graphical tool to C#
  - internal restructuring where architecture had previously been weak or absent
  - UI modernization from an outdated Windows 3.1 look to a more modern flat design
- The resulting value:
  - easier hiring and onboarding
  - faster and lower-cost changes
  - improved developer efficiency
  - software perceived as more modern by customers and developers
  - faster, less error-prone work in the UI

## Narrative Strategy

### Main Story The Homepage Must Tell
- Enrico is an experienced architect-minded technical leader.
- He has spent over 25 years in a demanding industrial domain.
- He now wants to bring that depth, quality mindset, and calm handling of complexity into a new environment.
- He is particularly valuable where architecture, quality, and human collaboration all matter.

### Narrative Sequence
- Start with who he is and what value he brings now.
- Quickly establish target role and professional level.
- Show how he thinks and works.
- Back up claims with selected proof points from real projects.
- Offer optional deeper reading for people who want more evidence.
- End with a clear invitation to discuss architect or lead roles.

## Content Strategy

### Content Priorities
- Prioritize clarity over completeness.
- Prioritize explicit positioning over biography trivia.
- Prioritize proof of judgment over long lists of technologies.
- Prioritize scannability for recruiters while preserving depth for expert readers.

### Content To Keep
- Personal tone
- Reflection on work style and values
- Concrete work samples
- Side-project proof
- Contact flow that does not expose the email immediately

### Content To Rewrite
- Hero copy
- Education / background section
- Role labeling near the top
- Work-sample framing so that business value and architectural impact are clearer
- Section-level narrative so the page feels intentionally structured for hiring

### Content To Reduce
- Overly specific nostalgia or details that do not help current positioning
- Tool lists that do not strengthen the hiring narrative
- Long prose that can be replaced with scannable summaries

### Content To Avoid
- Positioning that suggests pure individual-contributor coding as the main goal
- Positioning that suggests heavy people management as the current primary goal
- Messaging that keeps Enrico narrowly tied to industrial automation only
- Generic self-praise without evidence

## Education and Background Strategy

### Problems In The Current Version
- The first background paragraph is too specific in a way that no longer helps most visitors.
- Mentioning `KC85/1` is historically accurate but too obscure for today’s audience.
- The education section is too brief overall and does not fully support the desired positioning.
- The third paragraph overlaps with work-sample territory and should be rebalanced.

### Target Direction
- Keep the physics degree because it signals analytical depth.
- Keep the long professional history because it signals maturity and resilience.
- Remove or generalize obscure details that do not help current hiring goals.
- Reframe the section so it supports the transition story:
  - strong technical foundation
  - long experience in a demanding domain
  - now moving toward a new area of application

## Homepage Information Architecture

### Required Structure
- One single main homepage
- Multiple subpages for deeper project write-ups
- Navigation from homepage to subpages
- Navigation from subpages back to homepage

### Recommended Homepage Section Order
1. Hero
2. Positioning summary
3. Value / working style
4. Selected proof points or key achievements
5. Background and domain transition
6. Work samples
7. Side projects
8. Contact / closing CTA

### Notes On Structure
- The redesign does not have to preserve the exact current section order.
- It should preserve the overall split between quick homepage summary and deeper supporting pages.
- The homepage should feel more intentional and recruiter-oriented than the current sequence.

## Homepage Section Requirements

### 1. Hero
- Must immediately communicate:
  - name
  - target role identity
  - seniority / experience
  - concise value proposition
- Must include a clear primary CTA for contact.
- May include a short supporting line about current location and preferred work setup.

### 2. Positioning Summary
- Short section or block near the top.
- Should summarize:
  - architect and team lead orientation
  - years of experience
  - industrial depth plus transition to new areas
  - preferred kind of product work

### 3. Value / Working Style
- Should explain how Enrico works with complexity, people, and quality.
- This section may reuse some ideas from the current values section.
- It should be more clearly tied to hiring value than the current version.

### 4. Proof / Achievements
- Must present the three core proof points:
  - green-field architecture
  - quality practices
  - brown-field modernization
- Each proof point should be short, scannable, and outcome-oriented.
- The reader should understand both responsibility and impact.

### 5. Background / Transition
- Should cover:
  - physics education
  - long professional experience
  - current identity as architect and team lead
  - direct statement about seeking a new domain direction
- This section should not read like a full chronological CV.

### 6. Work Samples
- Should stay on the homepage.
- Should show concrete professional examples.
- Each item should make business impact and personal contribution easier to grasp than in the current version.

### 7. Side Projects
- Should stay as optional deeper proof.
- Should be framed as evidence of curiosity, standards, and self-driven learning.
- Teaser text should explain why each project matters, not just what it is.

### 8. Contact / Closing
- Should reinforce the invitation to discuss architect or technical-lead opportunities.
- Should keep the current low-friction contact mechanism unless a better simple solution is chosen.

## Subpage Strategy
- Side-project detail pages may remain article-style.
- Their purpose is supporting proof, not first-impression positioning.
- They should remain reachable from the homepage and easy to exit.
- They may be lightly rewritten later for consistency, but they are not the primary strategic problem.

## Visual Direction

### Brand Impression
- Light
- Optimistic
- Calm
- Mature
- Technically credible
- More distinctive than the current version

### Design Intent
- The site should not look like a generic developer portfolio.
- It should feel crafted, thoughtful, and clear.
- It should balance professionalism with personality.
- It should remain easy to comprehend quickly.

### Content Presentation
- Important claims should be easy to scan.
- Use stronger hierarchy and chunking than the current version.
- Reduce long uninterrupted paragraphs where possible.
- Make proof points visually legible and comparable.

## Tone of Voice
- Personal but not overly casual
- Reflective but not vague
- Calm and confident without self-marketing exaggeration
- Clear enough for non-technical readers
- Specific enough for technical readers to trust

## Interaction Requirements
- User can navigate from the main page to each side-project detail page.
- User can navigate from each side-project detail page back to the homepage.
- The main homepage CTA remains contact-oriented.
- Contact CTA must work with mouse and keyboard.
- If the reveal-email interaction is preserved:
  - email stays hidden until interaction
  - click and keyboard activation must work
  - revealed result becomes an actual `mailto:` link
- Interactive behavior should remain simple and robust.

## Accessibility and Usability Requirements
- Fast comprehension is mandatory.
- Main claims must be understandable without reading every paragraph.
- Images require alt text.
- Sections require clear headings.
- Layout must work on smaller screens.
- Keyboard access must be preserved for interactive elements.
- Visual contrast and spacing must support comfortable reading.

## Technical Requirements
- Simple HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
- No additional UI frameworks
- No CSS preprocessors
- Folder structure follows the page hierarchy
- No build step required for the portfolio site itself

- Existing homepage structure in `index.html`

- Homepage-specific rules in `index.css`
- Existing work-sample images
- Existing side-project preview images
- Existing side-project detail pages

- A recruiter can tell quickly that Enrico is being positioned for architect and technical-lead roles.
- A technical hiring manager can see real proof of architectural judgment and quality discipline.
- The homepage no longer depends on implication to communicate career direction.
- The transition from industrial automation to new areas is explicit and positive.
- The page attracts the right conversations and filters out obviously wrong-fit roles.
